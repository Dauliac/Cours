Monorepo vs Polyrepo

  • The organization of repositories is a crucial aspect of CI/CD.
  • Splitting or not splitting projects will determine the ease of adopting tools and practices‚Ä¶

Polyrepo

  • Advantages:
    1. Isolation and independence between projects.
    2. Easier management of individual repositories.
    3. Improved performance and scalability, but more complex maintenance.
  • Disadvantages:
    1. Harder project coordination.
    2. Risk of inconsistency and divergence.
    3. Tedious maintenance with multiple repositories.

Monorepo

  • Advantages:
    1. Easier collaboration and code sharing.
    2. Centralized version and security management.
    3. Possibility of frequent merge conflicts and complex dependencies.
  • Disadvantages:
    1. Frequent merge conflicts.
    2. Errors in one project can impact the entire system.
    3. More complex management as it scales.
